02 / COUNTERMEASURES — FIGHT BACK
STEP 01 / 06
🚫 Block Third-Party Cookies

Access your browser settings and disable all third-party cookies. This cuts off the primary cross-site tracking pipeline. Regularly clear your first-party cookies too — don't let stale data linger.

Chrome Settings Firefox Safari ITP Cookie AutoDelete
STEP 02 / 06
🦁 Switch to a Privacy Browser

Replace Chrome with Brave (blocks fingerprinting & ads by default) or Firefox with hardened settings. These browsers give you built-in protection without installing extras. Avoid Chromium-based browsers that phone home to Google.

Brave Browser Firefox Tor Browser LibreWolf
STEP 03 / 06
🛡 Install Tracking Blockers

Add uBlock Origin to your browser to stop tracking scripts, pixels, and ad networks before they load. It blocks at the network level — meaning the invisible tracking code never even runs on your device.

uBlock Origin Privacy Badger Ghostery AdGuard
STEP 04 / 06
🔒 Use a VPN

A VPN masks your real IP address, hiding your physical location and internet provider from trackers. Choose a no-logs VPN (Mullvad or ProtonVPN) — never free VPNs, which often sell the very data you're trying to protect.

Mullvad VPN ProtonVPN IVPN WireGuard
STEP 05 / 06
🔎 Use a Private Search Engine

Google logs every search query tied to your profile. Switch to DuckDuckGo, Brave Search, or Startpage — they return quality results without building a behavioral dossier on your curiosity and interests.

DuckDuckGo Brave Search Startpage SearXNG
STEP 06 / 06
📱 Harden Mobile & IoT

Audit app permissions — revoke location, microphone, and contacts access for apps that don't need them. Disable ad IDs on iOS and Android. For smart home devices like Alexa, review privacy settings or consider air-gapping them from sensitive conversations.

App Permissions Limit Ad Tracking DNS Firewall GrapheneOS
